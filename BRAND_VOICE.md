## Market: Hawai'i
**Entity:** Clever Wolf Digital

### Avatar -- DRAFT
**Name:** Kimo Akana _(DRAFT -- correct this)_
**Profile:** Mid-40s, owns a local trades or food business, been in the community his whole life. Skeptical of mainland tech hype. Word-of-mouth is everything. Not anti-tech, just burned before.

### Villain / Victim / Vow
- **Villain:** Mainland consultants who overpromise, take the check, and vanish after the kickoff call.
- **Victim:** Kimo is grinding 60-hour weeks while watching his cousin's business grow with tools he doesn't understand and doesn't have time to learn.
- **Vow:** We automate the stuff that keeps you stuck so you can stay in your lane and grow without burning out.

### Vocabulary Kimo actually uses
`busy season` / `my guys` / `we stay` / `no rush but...` / `real talk` / `what's the cost though` / `I heard about that` / `who set that up for you`

### Tone rules
- Talk story first, sell second.
- Practical over visionary. What does this do for me next week?
- No jargon unless you define it in the same sentence.
- Respect is shown through directness, not enthusiasm.
- Short sentences. Breathing room.

## Market: Seattle
**Entity:** Clever Wolf AI (Washington LLC)

### Avatar -- DRAFT
**Name:** Priya Nair _(DRAFT -- correct this)_
**Profile:** Late 30s, founder or ops lead at a 5-20 person professional services firm. Data-literate. Already lives in Notion/Slack/HubSpot. Impatient with anything that feels half-baked or over-pitched.

### Villain / Victim / Vow
- **Villain:** Generic AI consultants who deliver a ChatGPT wrapper and call it a system.
- **Victim:** Priya's team is smart but scattered. Every new tool creates more meetings, not fewer. She's the one who ends up holding the pieces together.
- **Vow:** We build the actual ops layer, not the slide deck about it.

### Vocabulary Priya actually uses
`bandwidth` / `async` / `ship it` / `what's the ROI` / `let's scope that` / `we need a system for this` / `I don't have cycles for that` / `can you send a Loom`

### Tone rules
- Lead with the outcome, not the process.
- Be specific. Numbers, timelines, deliverables. Vague is disqualifying.
- Peer-to-peer, not consultant-to-client.
- Dry humor is fine. Hype is not.
- One idea per sentence. No stacking.
