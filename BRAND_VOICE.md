Shared voice + targeting for Clever Wolf Digital AI offers.
Referenced by: OFFERS_STATE.md, PLAYBOOK.md, HOOKS.md. Hermes pulls voice from here.

GLOBAL GUARDRAILS
- No em dashes.
- Talk like the avatar's friend, in their words — not agency jargon.
- Sell the fix, not the feature. Clarity over cleverness.
- Lead with the pain on landing pages (warm); lead with disruption on ads (cold).

---

## MARKET 1 — HAWAI'I (Clever Wolf Digital)

AVATAR
A younger owner, 30s, running a local small business (mix of trades, service,
retail, food). Comfortable with tech but has zero time. Gets almost all business
by referral and word of mouth — barely has an online presence. Curious about AI,
knows it matters, but has no idea where to start. Not skeptical, just unsure and
busy. Local-first: trusts people who feel like they're from here, not a mainland
agency.

VILLAIN
No consistent marketing. The business runs on word of mouth and referrals, so
growth is unpredictable — busy one month, quiet the next. Marketing only happens
when they remember, then dies the moment a job gets busy. No system, no follow-up,
leads slip.

VICTIM (before)
Doing everything themselves. Marketing is the thing that always falls off the
plate. They know they're leaving money on the table but can't add one more task.

VOW (after)
Hours back. The busywork and follow-up run themselves. Marketing finally happens
consistently in the background without them lifting a finger, so referrals turn
into a real pipeline.

VOCABULARY (real phrases they'd use)
- "I just don't have time for it"
- "It's all word of mouth right now"
- "I know I should be doing more online"
- "I don't even know where to start with this AI stuff"
- "I wear all the hats"
- "If it actually saves me time, I'm in"

## MARKET 2 — SEATTLE (Clever Wolf AI, WA LLC)

AVATAR
Local service and retail business owners (similar profile to Hawai'i, not SaaS
founders). They know AI matters and that competitors will use it, but they have
no time and no in-house team to implement it. They want it done for them, not a
course or a tool to learn. Slightly more tech-comfortable market overall, but the
core need is the same: someone to build the system so they don't have to.

VILLAIN
Knows AI matters but has no time or team to implement it. Stuck watching the
opportunity go by — reads about it, maybe dabbles in ChatGPT, but nothing
connected ever gets built. The gap between "should be doing this" and "actually
doing this" keeps widening.

VICTIM (before)
Aware and a little anxious about falling behind. Has tools but they're scattered
and disconnected, or has nothing set up at all. No bandwidth to fix it.

VOW (after)
A connected system that runs without them. One brain wired into their business
that handles the work in the background, so a small team punches above its weight
and they stop falling behind.

VOCABULARY (real phrases they'd use)
- "I know I need to be doing this"
- "I don't have the team to set it up"
- "I've messed around with ChatGPT but that's it"
- "Everything's disconnected"
- "I just want it built for me"
- "I don't want to fall behind on this"

---

NOTE: Both markets target the SAME core buyer — a time-starved local business
owner who wants AI done FOR them, not taught to them. Seattle is slightly more
AI-aware; Hawai'i needs more local trust and hand-holding. Same offer ladder,
different emphasis.
