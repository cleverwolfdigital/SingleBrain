    Getting Started

    How to Use Single Brain

    Your command center for every business, project, client, and decision. Read the guide below, or take the guided tour.

    Getting in
    1

    Signing in

    Go to brain.cleverwolfdigital.com and enter your email. We send a one-time magic link — click it, then enter the 6-digit code from your authenticator app. First time only: scan the QR to set up Google or Microsoft Authenticator (links are on that page for iOS & Android).

    2

    Stay signed in

    On the code screen, leave “Remember this device for 90 days” checked so you're not logging in constantly. Access is limited to @cleverwolfdigital.com accounts (plus any guests an admin invites). Use the log-out icon (top-right) on shared devices.

    3

    Skip the code with a passkey

    Tired of opening your authenticator? Click your initials (top-right) → Add passkey and use Face ID, Touch ID, or Windows Hello. After that, just hit Sign in with Face ID / Touch ID on the login page — one touch, no email link and no 6-digit code. Add one per device you use. Your authenticator app still works as a backup, and a passkey is actually safer than a typed code: it only works on the real dashboard, so it can’t be phished.

    4

    Make it readable

    Text too small? Use the A− / A+ buttons (top-right) to grow or shrink the whole dashboard — your choice is remembered on this device.

    Finding your way
    5

    The sidebar

    Everything lives in the left sidebar. Overview is today's brief. Businesses, Projects, Clients, and Staff are your portfolio. Productivity reports on your time. Daily Journal, CWD Brain Chat, and Daily Rotation support the daily flow. To report a bug or idea, use the Feedback tab on the right edge of the screen (above the chat wolf).

    6

    Pin your favorites

    Open any business, project, campaign, or client and tap the ★ star to pin it to a Pinned section at the top of the sidebar (up to 5). Tap the star again to unpin. Great for the handful of things you touch every day.

    7

    Quick tools

    The Overview page has one-click shortcuts to ChatGPT, Claude, Grok, OpenClaw, and Hermes, plus your upcoming Google Calendar events (once your Drive is connected). The top bar has a Sync button (⟳) that pushes your latest to the shared brain, and a What's new button that shows the latest release notes — use ← → to browse past updates.

    Your portfolio
    8

    Businesses & tiers

    Businesses lists the whole portfolio grouped Tier 1–4 by priority. Click any card to open its profile — an overview plus its tasks grouped by priority. From the profile you can + Add task, ★ pin, and (admins) ✎ edit or 🗑 delete.

    9

    Sub-businesses

    Businesses can roll up under a parent. Example: Level Up Corporation shows its own card, and directly beneath it a boxed group of its sub-businesses (Level Up Self, Smarter, Library, The Smarter Podcast) — each a full card you can open.

    10

    Projects & campaigns

    Projects shows active work with status, owner, and the next concrete move. Filter by Active / Blocked / Parked. Campaigns (marketing pushes) appear in their own section. Click any card for its execution view.

    11

    Clients & retainers

    Clients tracks monthly retainers. Open a client to see its recurring monthly tasks. Admins can add a client, set the retainer, and attach recurring tasks (with a day of month + assignee) that auto-generate a fresh task every month.

    Getting work done
    12

    Quick Add

    The Quick add button (top bar) is a menu: Add a Business, Campaign, Project, Employee, or Task. Everyone can add tasks; the catalog items are admin-only. On a task, pick a business, category, priority, due date, estimate, and link dependencies.

    13

    Timers & the Action Queue

    Tasks live in the Action Queue (Projects page). Hit ▶ Start to run a live timer, ⏸ Pause to bank time, and ✓ to complete. Each task shows tracked-time / estimate so you can see where the hours go.

    14

    Productivity reports

    Productivity rolls up completed work by Day / Week / Month / Quarter / Year — tasks completed, time tracked, estimate accuracy, and breakdowns by business and category. Use ‹ › to move between periods.

    15

    Daily Journal

    Open Daily Journal each morning to set your one focus and top 3 priorities, and at end of day to log wins, decisions, and tomorrow's first action.

    16

    CWD Brain Chat

    Ask CWD Brain Chat (sidebar, or the floating wolf icon bottom-right) anything about your portfolio — priorities, a business, a project, what's overdue. It reads your live portfolio and tasks.

    17

    Files & attachments

    Attach files to any business, project, campaign, or task — for reference, review, or storage. Open the item and click Files, or use the 📎 on a task row. The first time, click Connect Your Drive — a window pops up to authorize Google, then closes on its own. Then upload a file (or drag & drop), add a link, or share any file by link or with a specific person. Each file has two removal options: the ✕ takes it off this item but keeps it in Drive, and 🗑 Delete from Google Drive moves the actual file to your Drive trash. A 📎 badge on each card shows how many files are attached.

    18

    Team Calendar & scheduling

    The Team Calendar sits right on your Overview — it opens on your own day; the dropdown switches to any teammate, and you can switch between Day and Week. Collapse it with the chevron if you'd rather not see it, or hit Full view (or the sidebar's Team Calendar) for the big version. The first time, click Reconnect Google (we added calendar access). To see someone's events, they share their Google Calendar with you first (Google Calendar → Settings → Share with specific people → "See all event details"). Click New meeting to create a Google Calendar event and email invites to teammates and clients you add.

    Team & admin
    19

    Super Admin

    Super Admin (sidebar, admins only) manages your team. Add members or guests by email, set each person's role, and grant access to specific businesses and projects — staff only see what you share with them.

    20

    Assigning work

    In Super Admin, the Assign tasks table lets you set who owns each open task. Assignees always see their tasks, even without full business access. You can promote up to 2 people to Super Admin.

    21

    One shared brain

    Everything syncs to a shared repository the whole team (and our AI tools) works from. It auto-syncs morning and evening; hit Sync (⟳) anytime to push immediately. Found a bug or have an idea? Use the Feedback tab.
