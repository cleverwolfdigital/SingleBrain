## Lesson 1: The Loop (the one idea that changes everything)

**The problem:** Most people use AI in open-ended bursts. Chat, get answers, close the tab. Next time: start over, re-explain everything, get slightly different answers. Effort in, nothing accumulates. It feels productive. It compounds into nothing.

**The idea:** People who build real things with AI run on *closed loops*. A loop means every work session ends by feeding its results back into the starting point of the next one. Work → save → update the plan → next session starts warm. Around and around, each pass building on the last.

**The metaphor to remember:** open-ended effort is a leaky bucket. A loop is a water wheel — the same water keeps doing work.

Everything in this course is a loop: how you work with AI (the Session Loop), how you keep your system healthy (the Review Loop), and eventually how you launch what you build (the Launch Loop).

**Action step (2 min):** Write down the ONE thing you want AI to help you build. A course, an ebook, a content series, a business plan — one thing. That's your project for the rest of this course.

## Lesson 2: The Map (how AI actually knows things)

You don't need to be technical. You need exactly two facts:

**Fact 1: The AI's built-in knowledge is frozen.** It learned from vast amounts of text months ago. It knows what a marketing funnel is. It knows nothing about you, your business, or your project. You cannot change this part.

**Fact 2: Everything the AI knows about YOU comes through one door — the context window.** That's the workspace holding your message, your uploaded documents, and your instructions. When the conversation ends, the door closes and the room empties.

Put those together and you get the master key: **working well with AI isn't about clever prompts. It's about controlling what goes through the door.**

Which leads to the most important rule in this course:

**Own your source of truth.** The documents that define your project — your plan, your audience, your voice — must live in files YOU own (Google Drive, your computer, Notion). The AI reads *from* your truth. It is never *itself* your truth. AI tools change, chats get lost, outputs drift. Your files are forever.

**Action step (3 min):** Create one folder on your computer or Drive named after your project. That folder is now the official home of everything you build. (That's it. Seriously.)

## Lesson 3: Don't Read Faster — Recontextualize

This lesson exists because of a mistake I was making, and you're probably making it too.

AI produces mountains of text. My instinct was to read all of it carefully so I wouldn't miss anything. Twenty minutes in, I was exhausted, behind, and anxious. Sound familiar?

**The fix isn't reading faster. It's making the text smaller before you read it at all.** The AI that produced the mountain can reshape the mountain. Just ask:

- *"Summarize that in 4 lines."*
- *"Dumb this down."*
- *"Just give me the decisions I need to make."*
- *"What changed since last time?"*

**Then apply the Read / Skim / Skip rule:**
- **Read carefully:** anything you'll publish, and any decision waiting on you.
- **Skim:** explanations. If the first line makes sense, move on.
- **Skip:** housekeeping ("I updated the file..."). Your system records everything — missing a paragraph costs you nothing.

**The mindset shift:** in an AI partnership, you are the *decision-maker*, not the proofreader. If you read everything deeply, you become the bottleneck in your own system.

**Action step (1 min):** In your next AI conversation, before reading a long reply, send: "Summarize that in 4 lines, decisions first." Feel the difference.

## Lesson 4: Build Your Blueprint

Your project needs a small set of documents that define its shape. I call this the **blueprint**. Here's the crucial distinction:

- **The blueprint** = the few, stable documents that define everything (your plan, your audience, your voice). Small. Lives in your folder AND gets given to the AI.
- **The building** = everything you produce (drafts, finished pieces, scripts). Large, always growing. Lives ONLY in your folder.

Mixing these up is how AI systems quietly break. The blueprint stays on the architect's table; you don't store the bricks there.

**Your blueprint needs just 3 documents to start** (use the templates in the Appendix):

1. **Master Plan** — what you're building, for whom, the pieces it needs, and a simple tracker of what's done. This is your most important file.
2. **Audience & Voice** — who this is for, and how you sound (3 examples of your writing beats any description).
3. **Decisions Log** — a running list of choices you've made, dated. ("Jul 7: title is X." "Jul 9: cut chapter 3.") This prevents the AI from re-litigating settled questions.

**Then set up your AI workspace** (in Claude, this is a "Project"; other tools have equivalents): upload your 3 blueprint documents, and write 3-5 sentences of standing instructions: *"This project builds [thing] for [audience]. Match my voice file. Check the Master Plan before suggesting structure changes. Flag contradictions instead of silently resolving them."*

Those five minutes of instructions are the highest-leverage five minutes in this entire system.

**Action step (20 min):** Fill in the three templates from the Appendix for your project. Rough is fine — the Loop will refine them.

## Lesson 5: The Session Loop (the heart of the system)

Here's how you'll work from now on. **One work session = one piece.** Not a five-hour mega-chat. You sit down, produce one thing (a chapter, a lesson, an email sequence), and then close the loop with four small moves — five minutes total:

**Move 1 — Save the work.** Copy what you finished into a file in your project folder. It lives with you, not in a chat.

**Move 2 — Update the Master Plan.** Mark the piece done in your tracker. Add any decisions to the Decisions Log. (You can ask the AI to write the update FOR you: "Give me the updated tracker lines and decision log entries from this session.")

**Move 3 — Refresh the AI's copy.** If the Master Plan changed, re-upload it to your AI workspace, replacing the old one. Skip this and the AI keeps working from a stale plan — the #1 way these systems quietly break.

**Move 4 — Write the briefing.** Ask the AI: *"Write me a 3-line briefing: what got done, what changed, what's next."* Save it. That briefing is your FIRST message next session. You start warm, every time.

**Save → update → refresh → briefing.** That's the whole loop. Do it after every session and you will never start from scratch again — not next week, not next year.

**Action step (one full session):** Do one real work session on your project and close it with all four moves. This is the course's most important action step.

## Lesson 6: Keep It Alive (why these systems break, and yours won't)

AI systems don't break loudly. They rot quietly. Four ways it happens — and notice the fix for all four is a loop you already have:

1. **The stale blueprint.** Your plan changed in your head but not in the file the AI reads. It keeps confidently building from the old plan. *Fix: Move 3 of the Session Loop.*
2. **Dilution.** You uploaded everything "just in case," and now the AI pulls from the wrong documents. *Fix: blueprint in, building out (Lesson 4).*
3. **Contradiction drift.** Old decisions and new decisions both live in your files, and the AI can't tell which one wins. *Fix: the Decisions Log — newest dated entry wins.*
4. **Orphaned work.** Something great exists only inside an old chat, invisible to every new session. *Fix: Move 1 — everything exits the chat into your folder.*

Add one **Review Loop**: once a month, spend 15 minutes asking "what's built, what's next, what's changed?" and update your Master Plan to match reality. That's the whole maintenance plan.

**One more gift — you can't break it.** Keep old versions of your Master Plan (add the date to the filename before big changes). Every mistake becomes reversible. Work boldly.

**Action step (5 min):** Add a monthly "Review Loop — 15 min" reminder to your calendar. Right now, while you're thinking of it.

## Appendix: The Templates

### Template 1 — Master Plan
```
PROJECT: [name]
WHAT I'M BUILDING: [one sentence]
FOR WHOM: [one sentence]
DONE MEANS: [what finished looks like]

THE PIECES:
1. [piece] — [not started / in progress / done]
2. [piece] — [status]
3. [piece] — [status]

LAST UPDATED: [date]
```

### Template 2 — Audience & Voice
```
MY AUDIENCE: [who they are, what they struggle with, what they want]
MY VOICE: [3 adjectives]
SOUND LIKE THIS (paste 2-3 short samples of your real writing):
1. ...
2. ...
NEVER: [words/tones you avoid]
```

### Template 3 — Decisions Log
```
[date]: [decision made]
[date]: [decision made]
(Newest entry wins if two conflict.)
```

### Template 4 — The Session Close (paste at the end of every AI work session)
```
Let's close the loop on this session:
1. Give me the final version of what we produced, ready to save.
2. Give me updated tracker lines and any new Decisions Log entries.
3. Write a 3-line briefing: what got done, what changed, what's next.
```

### Template 5 — The Session Open (paste at the start of every session)
```
[paste your 3-line briefing from last time]
Today's session: [the one piece we're producing].
Check the Master Plan first and flag anything that contradicts it.
```
