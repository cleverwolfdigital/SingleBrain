## Business Plan
Kimchee #88 is a fast-casual Korean-inspired restaurant concept located in Hawai‘i, blending authentic Korean flavors with local island ingredients and fast-casual dining efficiency. The restaurant will specialize in kimchi-based dishes, Korean comfort food, and Hawai‘i-style fusion meals that appeal to both locals and tourists.

Based off the success of Kimchee #7 in Pearl City, Hawaii, the business will be able to leverage the incredibly tasty Meat Jun sauce, Kalbi and authentic Korean BBQ Flavors and 20+ years of recognition and familiarity.

The goal of Kimchee #88 is to build a recognizable food brand centered around bold flavors, fast service, and high-quality ingredients. The concept highlights the food as the core identity of the brand, incorporated into signature dishes and fusion menu items.

The new concept will also utilize state-of-the-art technology in order to bring these types of food to the forefront of a new generation.

Kimchee #88 will operate as a fast-casual restaurant, combining dine-in, takeout, and delivery.

**Mission Statement**  
To bring bold Korean flavors and island culture together through fresh kimchi-driven dishes that are fast, flavorful, and unforgettable.

**Vision**  
To grow Kimchee #88 into a recognizable Hawai‘i restaurant brand with multiple island locations.

**Business Name:** Kimchee #88  
**Industry:** Restaurant / Food Service  
**Location:** Kapolei, Hawai‘i  
**Business Model:** Fast Casual Korean / Local Fusion

Kimchee #88 will serve Korean dishes with a Hawai‘i twist. The brand will highlight house-made kimchi, meat jun sauces, and incorporate it into bowls, plates, and fusion menu items.

The restaurant will focus on:
- fast service
- consistent quality
- strong branding
- affordable pricing
- local ingredients when possible

The name Kimchee #88 helps to distinguish it’s own identity while linking to the original concept of Kimchee #7.

**Market Analysis**  
Hawai‘i has one of the most diverse food cultures in the United States. Korean cuisine is already widely accepted among locals and visitors.

Major market drivers include:
- large Asian population
- tourism demand for unique food experiences
- strong takeout and delivery culture
- popularity of comfort foods and fusion cuisine

**Target Customers**  
- Local Residents: People who regularly eat out and enjoy Asian and local fusion cuisine.
- Young Professionals: Customers seeking quick, flavorful meals during lunch or after work.
- Tourists: Visitors interested in authentic local dining experiences.
- Family and Groups: Customers who appreciate Korean food and bold flavors.

**Competitive Analysis**  
Competitors include Korean BBQ restaurants, Korean plate lunch restaurants, Asian fusion restaurants, poke shops and local food spots.

Kimchee #88 differentiates itself by focusing on:
- Kimchi-Centered Branding: While most restaurants treat kimchi as a side dish, Kimchee #88 makes it the centerpiece of the menu.
- Fast-Casual Efficiency: Customers receive restaurant-quality meals quickly.
- Modern Branding: Clean, modern restaurant aesthetic appealing to younger audiences.
- Local Fusion Menu: Incorporating Hawaiian ingredients and flavors.

**Menu Concept**  
The menu will highlight kimchi-based dishes and Korean comfort food. These will be determined based on the current and existing menu, with enhancements and tests based on popularity.

**Marketing Strategy**  
Marketing will focus on strong social media presence and local brand recognition.

**Social Media Platforms:** Instagram, TikTok, Facebook

**Influencer Marketing:** Partner with Hawai‘i food influencers, TikTok creators, and local restaurant reviewers. Quincy has a connection with media and influencers on the island to do a strong grand opening launch.

**Promotions:** grand opening specials, lunch combo specials, seasonal limited-time dishes

**Community Partnerships:** food festivals, local events

**Operations Plan**  
**Restaurant Layout:** Look between 1,500 - 2,000 sqft space. Typical fast-casual layout: ordering counter, kitchen prep line, seating area, takeout pickup section.

**Hours of Operation:** Monday – Sunday, 10:30 AM – 9:00 PM

**Staffing Plan:** Initial staffing structure: Restaurant Manager – 1, Kitchen Staff – 3 to 4, Front Counter Staff – 2, Dishwasher / Prep Cook – 1. Total employees: 7 to 8 staff members.

**Suppliers:** Potential suppliers include local produce farms, Korean food wholesalers, Hawai‘i restaurant distributors, Costco and Sam’s Club runs.
